about

Pete Nottage is a UK-based presenter, voiceover and writer.

You’ll have probably either seen or heard him on your TV. Or radio. Or the web. He’s everywhere. A bit like ringworm, in that respect.

He’s a continuity announcer at Channel 4. He’s the station voice of BBC 6Music and MUTV. He’s presented stuff for Smart and Sony Ericsson and has also hosted the inaugural Oxford Fashion Week. He does loads of stuff. And nearly all of it is half-decent. He’s even made a Doctor Who parody video which has gained over a million hits on YouTube – but he’s not one to brag.

voiceover

Creative. Versatile. Youthful. Warm.

From commercials to narrations, from deep and serious reads through to friendly, neighbourly styles, Pete’s done them all.

With a neutral UK voice and a tone that’ll make you feel all fuzzy inside, he’s done work for clients as varied as Sony Playstation, Peugeot and Noel Gallagher – and can adapt his voice to fit whatever project you want.

presenting

Sharp. Thoughtful. And with really nice hair.

Like most presenters, Pete is a man of many skills. These include walking, talking – and very occasionally doing a mixture of the two.

Fun, unique and engaging, his quirky and irreverent style can inject new life into any project. He can even do it either standing up or sitting down. Totally up to you.
